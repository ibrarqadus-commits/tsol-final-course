Modern Slavery & Ethical Policy

Last updated: October 2025

1. Introduction

The Streets of London ("we", "us", "our") is committed to conducting our business in an ethical and responsible manner and upholding human rights in accordance with the Modern Slavery Act 2015.

Although we operate primarily as an online educational and training provider, we recognise the importance of ensuring that our business and supply chains are free from slavery, servitude, forced labour, and human trafficking.

2. Purpose

This policy sets out our commitment to preventing modern slavery and promoting ethical practices in our business operations and relationships with suppliers, partners, and contractors.

3. Our Commitment

  • We do not tolerate modern slavery or human trafficking in any part of our business or supply chain.
  • We expect our contractors, suppliers, and business partners to uphold the same high ethical standards.
  • We commit to training and raising awareness of modern slavery risks among relevant staff and associates.
  • We implement internal controls to identify, assess, and manage potential risks of slavery or unethical practices.

4. Reporting Concerns

Anyone who suspects or identifies practices that may constitute modern slavery or unethical behaviour is encouraged to report the matter to us promptly. Reports can be made confidentially to:

5. Review and Monitoring

We will review this policy periodically and monitor our business practices to ensure ongoing compliance and continuous improvement in our ethical standards.

6. Responsibility

The principal of The Streets of London is responsible for ensuring that this policy is implemented and adhered to across all operations and training activities.

7. Updates

This policy may be updated from time to time. The "last updated" date reflects the most recent revision.
